Course Description

The history of architecture in India is a material narrative of aspirations, polities, and societies in the region. From the Indus Valley Civilization to the modernism of B.V. Doshi, architecture has defined periods, peoples, and regions across India. This course is a brief introduction to a history of five thousand years of architecture in India, providing a broad perspective on one aspect of material expression.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the history and evolution of architecture in India
  • Learn about significant architectural styles and periods
  • Study material, design, and cultural influences in Indian architecture
  • Analyze architectural developments from ancient to contemporary India
  • Gain insights into architectural heritage and preservation

Course Layout

  • Week 1: Introduction, Indus Valley Civilization, Early Historic Period - Wooden and Cave Architecture
  • Week 2: Beginnings of the Temple, Medieval Temples, Islamic Architecture
  • Week 3: Sultanate Architecture, Mughals and Rajputs, Princely States
  • Week 4: Neo-Classical, Indo-Saracenic, Revival Gothic, Art Deco, Modern, International and Contemporary
